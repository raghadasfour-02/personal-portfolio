Visit to the National Museum of the U.S. Air Force

November 2nd, 2024

B-2 Bomber

The Northrop Grumman B-2 Spirit was revolutionary in stealth technology, created to penetrate dense Soviet air defenses during the Cold War. It uses composite materials, radar-absorbent coatings, and a lack of tail surfaces to minimize radar cross-section. The B-2 Spirit, known for its striking, flying-wing design, was a true highlight. The stealth technology that makes the B-2 so unique is mind-blowing. Seeing the bomber up close really gave me an appreciation for its advanced radar-evading design, which allows it to carry out strategic bombing missions while remaining undetected by enemy radar. One of the coolest facts is that it has the ability to carry both conventional and nuclear bombs, making it one of the most versatile bombers in the U.S. arsenal. I was amazed by the aircraft's size, too — it's larger than you might expect from the photos, with a wingspan of over 172 feet.

Convair B-36 Peacemaker

The Convair B-36 Peacemaker is enormous! Standing under this plane, you really get a sense of how big it was — with a wingspan of 230 feet, it’s the largest piston-engine aircraft ever built. This bomber played a critical role during the Cold War by carrying nuclear bombs, and its ability to fly at long ranges made it an essential part of the U.S. Air Force's strategic deterrence capabilities. Inside, I could imagine the complexity of operating this massive beast, with six engines and four jet engines, making it one of the most powerful bombers ever designed. The B-36 was essentially the Air Force’s workhorse, operating for many years and carrying out missions that shaped military history.
