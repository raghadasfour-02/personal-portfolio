My First Backcountry Camping Experience

September 15th, 2024

I just wrapped up my first backcountry camping trip, and it was quite the experience. For three days, I canoed through Algonquin Provincial Park, camped at some beautiful sites, and tracked the whole thing with my Apple Watch Series 9. Here's how it went down.


Day 1: Saturday

We started the trip with a 54-minute canoe ride to our first portage, a 270-meter stretch. The portage was a bit of a workout, but we made it through without much trouble. After that, we paddled for about an hour to our first campsite on Tepee Lake.

The campsite itself was great. It had a barrel hanger for storing food (a must when camping in bear country), a nice bench for sitting around the fire, and an awesome campfire spot. It was the perfect setup for our first night in the wild.


Day 2: Sunday

On day two, we canoed for about 1 hour and 10 minutes to our second campsite. Unfortunately, I ran into some tech issues with the Apple Fitness Paddling option on my watch, which messed up my map and was pretty frustrating. The good part of the day came when we hit the beach at the campsite. We went for a swim in the lake, and it was honestly the best part of the day. The water was beautiful, and it was a great way to cool off after paddling.


Day 3: Monday

On the last day, we canoed back from Tom Thomson Lake to our starting point. The wind was against us, which made paddling harder than expected, but the weather was good. There was a light breeze and some clouds that gave us breaks from the sun.

We snacked on mini Pepsis and granola bars along the way, which kept us going. The paddling was tough, but we made it back to the starting point without any major issues.


Wildlife Encounters

The trip was full of wildlife sightings. I saw river otters, beavers, loons, frogs, a bald eagle, and a mouse. I also spotted a woodpecker, which was a cool surprise. At night, I heard an owl and a bat, though I didn't manage to catch a glimpse of either.
